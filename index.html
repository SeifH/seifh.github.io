    Autonomous Robot
    I worked collaboratively in a team of 5 students and programmed a Turtlebot to perform various tasks inside an arena. The project helped me gain an understanding of the design process, automation, and integration of real-world Mechatronics systems. Our team used C++, ROS, and robotic algorithms to implement the following:

    1. Implemented an autonomous exploration and mapping algorithm. The robot used biased random walk navigation with static obstacle avoidance

    2. Implemented the shortest path planning algorithm to locate objects in a static environment. Implemented an unsupervised learning algorithm that used OpenCV to detect features and match images on each object
    Wall Mountable UV Phone Sanitizer for Hospitals
    I worked collaboratively in a team of 5 students to design and test a proof of concept of a wall-mountable UV phone sanitizer for CleanSlate UV. The design reduced hospital-acquired infections and protected patients with compromised immune systems. The team followed an engineering design process that included:

    • Defining scope and requirements per client’s request
    • Performing literature review and conducting a market research study
    • Multiple iterations of conceptual design generation
    • Industrial design generation
    • Pilot tests, UX studies, mock-ups, and stress analysis to assess proof of concept
    • Industrial design generation
    • Weekly client meetings to review progress and schedule
    The solution killed 99.9% of infectious bacteria and had a payback period of 3.7 months.

    Heat Distribution Optimization on a Circuit Board
    Worked in a team of 4 students to determine the optimal configuration of heater blocks and heat sinks that minimizes the surface temperature on a circuit board. Our team applied heat transfer principles, conducted experiments, and modelled the surface temperature distribution of the circuit board using finite element methods on MATLAB
    Foot Pedal Operated Trash Bin
    My group optimized the design of the City of Toronto’s garbage bins by reducing the user applied input force and increasing the opening area of the lids. The team built a fully functional SolidWorks model to simulate and study the mechanism. A full kinematic analysis (position, velocity, and acceleration analysis) was performed using MATLAB. The redesigned mechanism reduced the input force by 35% and added 4,200 cm² to the opening area of the lids.
    Dovetail Joint Design in a Compressor Engine
    In a team of 6 students, I conducted extensive testing and analysis of a dovetail joint of an aero-engine compressor. The project was a prelude to understanding requirements for FAA certification. The team conducted the following tests and analysis:

    • FEA using ANSYS to identify high-stress concentrations
    • Strain gauges to measure the stress at the point of contact
    • Photoelasticity to verify the previous experimental results
    CNC Milling Machine
    In a 6-person team, I designed and built a fully functional SolidWorks model of an entry-level professional CNC Milling machine. Specifically, my role included a literature review, research and analysis of different mechanical components as well as building a 3D model and assembly of the Z-axis motion controls and mechanisms. The machine had a resolution of 2 µm and if it enters production, it was estimated to cost $1,200 (CAD).
    WaterLine: Water Conservation
    As part of the 2016 Sustainable Design Awards, I designed and prototyped in CAD a water-saving shower tank for the average household. My design cleaned and recycled greywater from showers and baths for reuse in toilet flushing. By re-rerouting water used in showers to the toilet cistern, it minimized water wastage and lowered costs associated with reduced water usage.

    Noise Abatement and Visual Privacy System
