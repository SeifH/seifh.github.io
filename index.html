CNC Milling Machine

Mechanical Engineering Design

The entry-level professional CNC milling machine designed and analyzed by a 6-person team in MIE243, Mechanical Engineering Design at the University of Toronto. The machine is to be used by small businesses for the manufacture of high-accuracy and high-quality prototypes. The machine has a resolution of 2 um and if it enters production, it is estimated to cost around $2,000 (CAD).

WaterLine

2016 Sustainable Design Awards

I invented and prototyped a product named WaterLine for the 2016 Sustainable Design Awards competition. The design aims to tackle sustainability issues that we encounter in our everyday lives. WaterLine is a water-saving shower tank for the average household. The system captures greywater from showers for reuse in toilet flushing. A poster was designed detailing the project and a prototype was produced in SketchUp.
